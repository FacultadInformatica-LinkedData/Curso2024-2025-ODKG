Ontology Specification Draft

Abstract

This ontology provides a comprehensive framework for representing air quality metrics, including concentrations of pollutants such as NOx, CO, PM2.5, PM10, and BTX, as well as the corresponding measurement values, timestamps, and metadata about monitoring stations and their sensors. The ontology aims to facilitate data interoperability and sharing among environmental monitoring systems, researchers, and policy-makers, enabling better analysis and understanding of air quality dynamics in urban and rural settings.

Introduction

Air pollution poses significant challenges to public health and the environment, necessitating accurate and timely monitoring of air quality metrics. The proliferation of air quality sensors and monitoring stations has generated vast amounts of data, which can be leveraged for research and policy-making. This ontology is designed to standardize the representation of air quality data, encompassing key metrics and contextual information related to monitoring stations and their respective sensors. By providing a structured approach to air quality data, the ontology promotes data sharing, integration, and analysis across different platforms and applications.

Overview

The air quality ontology includes several core components:

  • Air Quality Metrics: Definitions and classifications of various pollutants, including nitrogen oxides (NOx), carbon monoxide (CO), particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10), and benzene, toluene, and xylene (BTX). Each metric is associated with its concentration value, measurement units, and observation timestamp.
  • Monitoring Stations: Information about the geographical locations of air quality monitoring stations, including attributes such as latitude, longitude, altitude, and address. Each station can be linked to multiple sensors.
  • Sensors: Details about the sensors deployed at each monitoring station, including their types, operational status, and the specific metrics they measure. Sensors are essential for obtaining accurate and real-time air quality data.
  • Temporal Aspects: The ontology captures the temporal dimension of measurements, allowing for time-series analysis of air quality data over days, months, or years.

Description

The ontology is structured using established semantic web standards, including RDF (Resource Description Framework), OWL (Web Ontology Language), and RDFS (RDF Schema).

Key Classes and Properties

  • AirQualityMetric: Represents the measured value of a specific pollutant.
    • Properties: numericValue, measurementUnit, resultTime.
  • Station: Represents the physical location of an air quality monitoring station.
    • Properties: latitude, longitude, altitude, address.
  • Sensor: Represents the individual sensors deployed at a station.
    • Properties: sensorType, observes, madeBySensor.

The relationships between these entities are defined using object properties, facilitating queries about air quality data in relation to time, location, and sensor performance.

This ontology enables users to capture, query, and analyze air quality data effectively, enhancing understanding of air pollution trends and facilitating informed decision-making.
