Overview

The air quality ontology includes several core components:

  • Air Quality Metrics: Definitions and classifications of various pollutants, including nitrogen oxides (NOx), carbon monoxide (CO), particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10), and benzene, toluene, and xylene (BTX). Each metric is associated with its concentration value, measurement units, and observation timestamp.
  • Monitoring Stations: Information about the geographical locations of air quality monitoring stations, including attributes such as latitude, longitude, altitude, and address. Each station can be linked to multiple sensors.
  • Sensors: Details about the sensors deployed at each monitoring station, including their types, operational status, and the specific metrics they measure. Sensors are essential for obtaining accurate and real-time air quality data.
  • Temporal Aspects: The ontology captures the temporal dimension of measurements, allowing for time-series analysis of air quality data over days, months, or years.
