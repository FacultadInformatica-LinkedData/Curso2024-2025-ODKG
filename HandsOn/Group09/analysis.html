License

The data from the Madrid City Council do not have a clear license, such as Creative Commons, that simplifies use and redistribution. However, they do have well-defined terms of use that regulate how the data can be reused. While there isn’t a standard license mentioned, the data can still be reused under certain guidelines that include citing the source, not distorting the information, and prohibiting the re-identification of personal data.

1. Authorization for Reuse and Non-Exclusive Intellectual Property Rights Assignment
  • The reuse of data and documents is allowed for both commercial and non-commercial purposes.
  • Reuse includes activities such as copying, distributing, modifying, adapting, extracting, reorganizing, and combining the information, provided it is not used for public administrative purposes.
  • Documents refer to any data, in any format (material or electronic), including raw data at its most disaggregated levels.
  • A free, non-exclusive transfer of intellectual property rights is granted, allowing reproduction, distribution, public communication, or transformation of the information in any format and modality.
  • This license applies worldwide and for the maximum term allowed by law.
2. General Conditions for Reuse
  • The meaning of the information must not be distorted.
  • The source of the reused information must be cited: "Data source: Madrid City Council" or the relevant administrative body.
  • The date of the last update of the documents must be mentioned, if available.
  • It must not be suggested or implied that the Madrid City Council endorses or supports the reuse of the information.
  • Metadata regarding the update date and reuse conditions included in the original document must be preserved and not altered.
  • Re-identification of anonymized data is strictly prohibited for privacy protection.
Potential license: Creative Commons Attribution

Based on the conditions outlined by the Ayuntamiento de Madrid regarding data reuse, it’s clear that the municipality encourages the open use of its data while imposing specific requirements and restrictions to ensure responsible and ethical usage. For that reason, the most suitable license will be Creative Commons Attribution (CC BY), especially given the conditions set by the Ayuntamiento de Madrid. This license allows users to distribute, remix, adapt, and build upon the work, even commercially, provided they credit the original creation. aligning perfectly with the Ayuntamiento's citation requirement.

Dataset Analysis

datos_diarios_2023.csv

The air quality dataset from the Madrid City Council contains daily measurements of various pollutants at several stations distributed throughout the city, including data on contaminants and their validity. This dataset allows for the analysis of air quality trends in Madrid over time.

Key Columns:
  • PROVINCIA, MUNICIPIO, ESTACION: Identifiers for location and station.
  • MAGNITUD: Likely represents the type of measurement.
  • PUNTO_MUESTREO: The sampling point identifier.
  • ANO and MES: Year and month.
  • D01 to D31: Daily data for up to 31 days of the month.
  • V01 to V31: Validation flags for the corresponding day (D01 to D31), likely indicating whether the data is valid (V) or not (N).
Data Types:
  • Numeric columns: Most daily values (D01, D02, etc.) are floats, representing some continuous measurement.
  • Categorical columns: Columns like V01, V02, etc., contain validation flags (V for valid, N for invalid).
Further Analysis:
  • Count: Each daily measurement column has 1,566 entries, which means that the dataset is complete in terms of the daily values recorded.
  • Mean: The mean values range from 14.77 to 25.60 across different days. The values are relatively consistent, with some fluctuation depending on the day.
  • Min/max values: The minimum value for all days is 0. Maximum values reach as high as 354, indicating some outliers or high peaks in the data.

Resource Name Strategy

  • URI form (resources): http://mAIRdrid.org/{vX}/ontology/{ontologyName}#{className}
  • URI form (individuals): http://mAIRdrid.org/{vX}/resource/{className}/{identifier}
  • Identifiers: Identifiers are unique across both paths to avoid confusion.
  • URIs domain: mAIRdrid.org
  • Content negotiation: The ontology will support content negotiation to serve different formats such as RDF/XML, Turtle, and JSON-LD based on the client's request.
  • Pattern: {resourceType} should represent categories such as "AirQualityMetric", "Measurement", or "Station", while {identifier} will be a unique identifier (e.g., a UUID or a sequential number).
