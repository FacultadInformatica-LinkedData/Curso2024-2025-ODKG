Dataset analysis

Dataset analysis

  • General Overview:
    • The dataset consists of 80,189 records and 19 columns, representing traffic accidents in Madrid.
    • Each record provides details such as accident location, date, weather conditions, vehicle type, person type, age, gender, severity of injuries, and geographical coordinates.
  • Missing Data:
    • Significant missing data was found, especially in:
      • estado_meteorológico (9,340 missing entries).
      • cod_lesividad and lesividad (both with 36,940 missing entries), which record the severity of the accident.
      • Drug test results (positiva_droga): Almost entirely missing for both years (79,835 entries missing).
  • Categorical Variables:
    • District (distrito): There are 21 unique districts. The most accident-prone district is "Puente de Vallecas", with 6,383 cases.
    • Type of Accident (tipo_accidente): 13 different accident types are recorded, with "Colisión fronto-lateral" being the most common type (20,877 cases).
    • Weather Conditions (estado_meteorológico): Accidents mostly occur under "Despejado" (Clear) conditions, appearing in 60,724 cases.
    • Vehicle Type (tipo_vehiculo): There are 35 unique vehicle types. "Turismo" (Private car) is the most frequent vehicle involved, making up 55,150 cases.
    • Person Type (tipo_persona): "Conductor" (driver) is the predominant role, with 64,359 cases.
    • Age Range (rango_edad): 18 distinct age ranges are represented. A large number (8,747) of entries are marked as "Desconocido" (Unknown).
    • Gender (sexo): "Hombre" (male) accounts for 48,424 cases, while "Mujer" (female) is recorded in 31,765 cases.
  • Lesividad (Severity of Injury):
    • Severity of accidents is mostly low:
      • "Sin asistencia sanitaria" (No medical assistance) is the most common outcome, occurring in 24,649 cases.
      • Serious injuries leading to "Fallecido 24 horas" (Death within 24 hours) occurred in 45 cases.
      • Other cases include different levels of medical attention, such as immediate attention at the scene, hospitalization, or emergency care.
      • 4 cases have unknown severity ("Se desconoce").
  • Numerical Data Overview:
    • The severity code (cod_lesividad) ranges from 1 to 77, with an average severity level of 10.2.
    • Geographical Coordinates (coordenada_x_utm and coordenada_y_utm) mostly represent locations concentrated around Madrid, with some entries containing zero values indicating missing or incorrect data.
  • Summary Insights:
    • Most accidents occur in clear weather, with private cars being the most involved vehicle type.
    • Males represent the majority of those involved in accidents, and drivers are more commonly reported than passengers or pedestrians.
    • Low severity incidents dominate the dataset, with a small percentage of severe injuries or fatalities.
    • The data contains significant gaps, particularly in drug test results and accident severity, which may limit certain types of analysis.

Resource Naming Strategy

Domain:

Use a custom domain relevant to your dataset, e.g., http://accidents.madrid.es/.

Classes and Properties:

Use different paths for ontology resources such as classes (/ontology/) and properties (/properties/):

  • Classes:
    • http://accidents.madrid.es/ontology/Accident
    • http://accidents.madrid.es/ontology/Street
    • http://accidents.madrid.es/ontology/Vehicle
  • Properties:
    • http://accidents.madrid.es/properties/hasLocation
    • http://accidents.madrid.es/properties/involvesVehicle
    • http://accidents.madrid.es/properties/occurredOnDate

Individuals: Use different paths for individuals, ensuring they are distinct for different classes:

  • Accidents: http://accidents.madrid.es/resources/accident/{AccidentID}
  • Streets: http://accidents.madrid.es/resources/street/{StreetName}
  • Vehicles: http://accidents.madrid.es/resources/vehicle/{VehicleID}
