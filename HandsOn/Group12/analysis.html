Dataset analysis

Dataset Overview

  • Records: 31,359 traffic accidents in Madrid (2024).
  • Columns: 21 attributes, including accident location, date, weather conditions, vehicle type, person type, age, gender, severity, and geographic coordinates.

Missing Data

  • Minimal missing data in the new dataset:
    • **tipo_vehiculo**: 243 missing entries
    • **coordenada_x_utm** and **coordenada_y_utm**: 2 missing entries each

Categorical Variables

  • Weather Conditions: "Despejado" (clear) dominates (most accidents happen in clear weather).
  • Vehicle Type: "Turismo" (private car) is the most involved vehicle (majority of accidents).
  • Person Type: "Conductor" (driver) remains the most common person involved in accidents.
  • Age Range: Most accidents involve people aged 25-34 years.
  • Gender: Males are more involved in accidents than females.

Severity of Accidents

  • The majority of accidents involve **"Sin asistencia sanitaria"** (no medical assistance).
  • Serious injuries are rare, with only a few deaths within 24 hours.
  • Most accidents are minor, with an average severity code of 40.2.

Summary Insights

  • Clear weather conditions and private cars are the leading factors in accidents.
  • Males, particularly drivers, remain the most commonly involved demographic.
  • Drug and alcohol test results show a very low positive rate.
